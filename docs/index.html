Choose your path

Three useful modes. No mystery switches.

Start with the lightest option that solves the problem. Quality controls and GPU-budget adaptation keep the workload practical on real hardware.

01 / NATIVE

Keep the pixels honest

Runs the video through the local rendering path without enhancement. Useful for comparison and experimental smoothing.

Baseline
02 / FSR1

Fast, clean upscaling

EASU reconstructs the image while RCAS restores crisp detail. The best starting point for most videos.

Recommended
03 / RAVU

More adaptive detail

RAVU-lite applies learned reconstruction before RCAS. It can reveal more structure when the GPU budget allows it.

Under the hood

A short path from video to screen.

Everything happens locally in a WebGL2 pipeline. The extension monitors timing and can reduce work when a chosen effect becomes too expensive.

01

Find the main video

The top-level page's primary HTML5 video becomes the source.

02

Sample each frame

The current frame is uploaded directly into the local GPU pipeline.

03

Apply the effect

FSR1 or RAVU-lite reconstructs; RCAS controls final sharpness.

04

Present and adapt

The enhanced frame is shown while timing guards protect playback.

Honest limits

Experimental means experimental.

Frame interpolation is not RIFE

The lightweight experimental path can show ghosting around occlusions, fast motion and scene cuts.

Some protected video cannot be captured

DRM restrictions and cross-origin policies can prevent local processing.

GPU cost still matters

RAVU and interpolation may be reduced or cancelled when they exceed the available rendering budget.

One main video at a time

Videos inside iframes and secondary players are intentionally outside the current scope.
